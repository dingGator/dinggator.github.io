Gainesville, Florida

Lush ferns painted the cavity wall with a lush green, and feeding off the water of the creeks. Needle palms and orchids were scattered with the ferns giving a beautiful wall to the sink hole.
The sinkhole was formed when the rainwater became slightly acidic as it seeps thru the dead plants layers on the ground. The slightly acidic water then dissolved the underlying limestone bedrock forming a huge cavern. Eventually when there not enough limestone to support the weight of the land above the ceiling of the limestone collapsed forming the sinkhole.
Even deer and turkey can be seen occasionally.
