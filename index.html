Gainesville, Florida

Devils Millhopper Geological State Park, National Natural Landmark, A sink hole that is 120 feet deep and 500 feet wide. The sink hole has a miniature rain forest. Small streams and fountains are just two of many features of the sink hole. Natural vegetation, shells, shark teeth and fossils remains line the walls and the sink for the casual naturalist in all of us.

This website will help you enjoy your visit to Devils Millhopper. It will highlight the history, tours, and activities of the Devils Millhopper Park. It will map out many must-see and not-to-be-missed parts of Devils Millhopper.
